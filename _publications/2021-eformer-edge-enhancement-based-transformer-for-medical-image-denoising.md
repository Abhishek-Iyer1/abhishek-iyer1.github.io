<p class="special">In this work, we present Eformer - Edge enhancement based transformer, a novel architecture that builds
an encoder-decoder network using transformer blocks for medical image denoising. Non-overlapping window-based 
self-attention is used in the transformer block that reduces computational requirements. This work further 
incorporates learnable Sobel-Feldman operators to enhance edges in the image and propose an effective way to concatenate 
them in the intermediate layers of our architecture. The experimental analysis is conducted by comparing 
deterministic learning and residual learning for the task of medical image denoising. To defend the effectiveness of our 
approach, our model is evaluated on the AAPM-Mayo Clinic Low-Dose CT Grand Challenge Dataset and achieves 
state-of-the-art performance, i.e., 43.487 PSNR, 0.0067 RMSE, and 0.9861 SSIM. We believe that our work 
will encourage more research in transformer-based architectures for medical image denoising using residual learning.</p>
